Void Arena

A wave-based combat game developed in Unity, focused on gameplay systems, enemy behaviour, and progression.

Project Overview

Void Arena is a wave-based combat game developed in Unity as part of my Games Development work. The project focuses on building a complete gameplay experience through the integration of core systems such as player movement, combat, enemy AI, progression, and feedback.

The player survives against increasing waves of enemies, with boss enemies introduced at regular intervals. The project was designed to combine technical implementation with clear gameplay progression and responsive controls.

Project Media

Demonstration of gameplay including combat, enemy behaviour, wave progression, and boss encounters.

Key Features

Movement

Camera-relative player movement with directional control and a dash mechanic.

Combat

Melee and ranged attacks implemented using hitboxes and projectiles.

Enemy AI

NavMesh-based enemy behaviour for movement, tracking, and combat pressure.

Wave Progression

Increasing enemy count and difficulty across waves to create a strong gameplay loop.

Boss System

Boss enemies introduced every five waves with increased scale and health.

UI & Audio

Health, wave, and cooldown feedback supported by sound effects and music.

Tools & Technologies

Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Systems Used: NavMesh, UI, audio, scene management

What I Learned

This project helped strengthen my understanding of gameplay programming, system integration, debugging, and balancing progression over time.

It also improved my ability to design modular systems that interact cleanly, such as movement, combat, health, enemy behaviour, and wave progression.
